## TL;DR

Two narrative personas built for content creation — Instagram posts, blog articles, carousels, and copy hooks written from the inside of their lives, not from the outside of a product pitch. Each persona has a character card, a day-in-their-life, their inner monologue, what stops their scroll, and content angle recommendations. Operational qualification data (pain inventory, desire inventory, objection bank) lives in the ICP packs — this file adds the human layer that makes content feel written by someone who actually knows them.

---

## When to use

- Writing Instagram posts, carousels, stories, or blog articles from the ICP's perspective
- Briefing a content creator or AI on the emotional register for a specific piece
- Writing copy that needs to feel like it was written by someone who understands clinic life
- Checking if a piece of content would resonate with Perfil A, Perfil B, or both

### Perfil A — Dona de Clínica com Equipe

---

### A Tuesday in Bê's Life

**7h00** — Phone buzzes. Three WhatsApp messages from the clinic number that her receptionist hasn't seen yet. One is a patient asking to reschedule. One is a new contact asking *"quanto custa a consulta?"* — arrived at 10pm the night before. She replies to the reschedule. Leaves the price question for the receptionist.

**8h30** — Arrives at the clinic. Her receptionist mentions that the Monday *"bate-papo"* with a new contact didn't show up. Third no-show this month on a first appointment.

**9h–12h** — Three sessions. She's fully present in each one. This is the part she loves. This is why she opened a clinic.

**12h15** — Lunch at her desk. Opens the spreadsheet. This week: 6 empty slots between the 5 psychologists. Tries to calculate what that's costing — loses track, closes the spreadsheet.

**13h** — Quick alignment with the receptionist. Goes over the week's schedule. Reminds her again about the WhatsApp follow-up protocol they tried to implement in February. It hasn't stuck.

**14h–18h** — Four more sessions.

**18h30** — On the way home, thinks: *"Semana que vem pode ser pior. Preciso fazer alguma coisa."* Doesn't know what to do that doesn't feel like she's selling therapy.

**21h** — Child asleep. Opens Instagram. Sees a competitor clinic in Campinas with a sleek site and 180 Google reviews. Feels a mix of admiration and unease. Closes the app.

---

### Inner Monologue — Her Voice

> *"A clínica tá bem — mas nunca sei como vai ser o próximo mês. Meses bons eu fico aliviada. Meses ruins eu fico me perguntando o que eu fiz diferente."*

> *"Minha recepcionista é boa, mas ela não tem processo. Ela improvisa. E eu não tenho tempo de treinar do jeito que precisa."*

> *"Odeio a palavra 'marketing'. Parece que estou vendendo terapia. Não é o que eu quero passar."*

> *"Já tentei anúncio uma vez. Veio bastante contato, mas ninguém converteu. Desconfio que o problema era outro."*

> *"Se eu tivesse uma agenda previsível — só isso — eu conseguia planejar minha vida."*

---

### What Stops Her Scroll

- *"Como eu reduzi o no-show da minha clínica de 40% para 8%"* — numbers + specific result
- *"O que fazer quando a recepcionista improvisa no atendimento"* — her exact daily problem
- *"Agenda previsível sem parecer agressiva comercialmente"* — her exact desire + her exact fear
- Before/after stories from clinic owners with similar profiles
- Posts that validate her caution: *"Crescer com cuidado não é covardia. É identidade."*
- Posts that name the invisible loss she feels: *"Você sabe quantas pessoas abriram seu site essa semana e não entraram em contato?"*

### What She Keeps Scrolling Past

- Posts that say *"escale seu negócio"* or *"fature mais"*
- Aggressive sales energy — countdown timers, *"vagas limitadas"*
- Testimonials with revenue screenshots — feels invasive to her professional identity
- Generic digital marketing content — she can tell immediately when someone doesn't know what a psychology clinic actually is

---

### Content Angles That Work for Bê

| Angle | Why it works | Example hook |
|---|---|---|
| The invisible loss | Names a problem she feels but can't quantify | *"Quantos pacientes chegaram até você essa semana e desistiram porque ninguém respondeu rápido?"* |
| Process, not sales | Reframes commercial structure as care | *"Confirmar a consulta com antecedência não é pressão. É cuidado."* |
| Validation of caution | Meets her where she is before offering anything | *"Você não precisa parecer uma clínica de marketing. Você precisa parecer o que você já é — só mais fácil de encontrar."* |
| The oscillation cost | Makes the monthly uncertainty visible | *"Mês bom, mês ruim, sem conseguir prever. Esse ciclo não é azar. É falta de processo."* |
| Practical clinic management | Demonstrates niche knowledge | *"O follow-up de confirmação não é para encher o saco do paciente. É para ele lembrar que a sessão importa."* |

### A Wednesday in Carol's Life

**8h00** — Checks WhatsApp. A message arrived last night at 8pm: *"Oi, vi seu Instagram e queria saber valores e disponibilidade."* She replies now. The person never responds.

**8h30–12h30** — Four sessions. She's focused, present, doing the work she trained for. No interruptions — phone on silent.

**12h30** — Opens WhatsApp. Two new contacts while she was in session. One asks the price — responds. The other sent a voice message she hasn't listened to yet. Eats while typing.

**13h** — Looks at her Google Maps profile. 3 reviews. The clinical center two blocks away has 61. She considers asking patients to leave a review, feels uncomfortable about it, closes the app.

**13h–17h** — Four more sessions.

**17h30** — Last patient leaves. She has 30 minutes before her commute. Opens Instagram, writes half a post about anxiety, decides it's not good enough, doesn't publish. Closes the app. Has not posted in 11 days.

**18h30** — On the subway, counts: 18 patients × R$160 × average 3 sessions/month = R$8.640. Thinks about raising to R$190. Immediately imagines someone canceling because of it. Keeps the number at R$160.

**21h** — Messages a colleague about a patient she referred last week. Thinks: *"Preciso indicar mais pra receber mais indicação. Não tem outro jeito."*

---

### Inner Monologue — Her Voice

> *"Quando saio da sala, sempre tem mensagem sem resposta. Já perdi paciente pra quem respondeu primeiro."*

> *"Cobro R$160 porque tenho medo de perder paciente se subir. Mas eu sei que tô cobrando abaixo do que deveria."*

> *"Meu Instagram tem engajamento mas nunca virou paciente de verdade. Não sei o que estou fazendo errado."*

> *"Sou a terapeuta, a secretária e a gestora ao mesmo tempo. Não sobrou espaço pra mais nada."*

> *"Não quero parecer que estou me vendendo. Psicóloga não faz isso."*

---

### What Stops Her Scroll

- *"Como apareci no Google sem gastar com anúncio"* — low risk, no-ad framing resonates immediately
- *"Como cobrei mais e não perdi nenhum paciente"* — her exact VPS fear addressed directly
- Posts validating solo practice: *"Trabalhar sozinha é difícil. Não porque você não é boa o suficiente."*
- *"A resposta que eu não dei enquanto estava em sessão"* — the invisible loss framing for solo
- Practical WhatsApp tips — she knows she's losing leads there
- NR-1 2026 context: companies needing to offer psychological support = new demand she can access

### What She Keeps Scrolling Past

- Content aimed at clinic owners with teams — creates distance
- *"Escale seu consultório"* — feels aggressive and disconnected from her reality
- Revenue flexing or case studies with big numbers — feels unreachable, not inspiring
- Long theoretical content about digital marketing — she doesn't want to learn marketing, she wants something to work for her

---

### Content Angles That Work for Carol

| Angle | Why it works | Example hook |
|---|---|---|
| The silent loss | Names what she knows is happening but can't see | *"Você não sabe quantos pacientes te encontraram no Google, não viram nada, e foram embora."* |
| VPS identity reframe | Addresses the belief before the number | *"Um iPhone por R$450 gera desconfiança. Sua consulta a R$130 pode estar fazendo o mesmo."* |
| Solo practitioner solidarity | Validates her reality before offering anything | *"Você é a terapeuta, a recepcionista e a gestora. Isso não é fraqueza. É o cenário que ninguém te preparou pra ter."* |
| Low-complexity entry | No team, no tech, configura uma vez | *"Uma coisa que você faz uma vez e funciona enquanto você atende."* |
| Mission framing | Connects commercial presence to helping more people | *"Aparecer no Google não é publicidade. É garantir que quem já está buscando ajuda consiga te encontrar."* |

## How to apply

- **Writing an Instagram post:** pick one persona, pick one content angle, write as if you're speaking directly to that person in that moment of her day. Use her inner monologue language as the opening hook.
- **Writing a carousel:** open with a loss statement (invisible or concrete), build through the emotional journey, close with a reframe — not a product pitch. The persona should feel seen, not sold to.
- **Writing a blog post:** use the day-in-life narrative as structure — the opening scene, the moment of friction, the insight. The product or process is never the protagonist; she is.
- **Choosing which persona to address:** if the content angle is about team, process, or predictable revenue → Bê. If the angle is about solo practice, VPS, Google visibility, or WhatsApp response → Carol. Some angles (the invisible loss, ethics + growth, mission framing) work for both.
- **AI-generated content:** load the relevant persona's inner monologue verbatim as the tone anchor. The first sentence of any AI-generated hook should mirror her voice, not a generic copywriter's voice.
