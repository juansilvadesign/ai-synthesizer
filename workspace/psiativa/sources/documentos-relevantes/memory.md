Key learnings & principles

Vocabulary is positioning: The ICP reacts negatively to "marketing" language due to prior bad experiences with traditional agencies. Replacing it with authority-construction and process vocabulary is a strategic differentiator, not just a stylistic choice.
Hemorrhage over improvement: Positioning that addresses quantifiable, urgent business risk (dependency on referrals, operational cash drain, competitive invisibility) outperforms copy that frames outcomes as incremental improvements.
Ethics-vs-commercial tension is the central objection: Content and sales materials must consistently resolve the perceived conflict between professional identity and commercial growth — this is the ICP's primary psychological barrier.
Benchmarking outside the niche: Insights from adjacent high-ticket niches (medicine, law) have been productively applied to PsiAtiva's positioning.
Proof before scale: Building concrete case studies before expanding outreach is a deliberate sequencing principle from the Fast Scale program.
