# Personal Branding

A three-layer skill that constructs a personal brand from the inside out: **Soul → Code → Body**. Each layer must be solid before the next is touched. Do not jump to visual or verbal expression (Layer 3) until Layers 1 and 2 are complete.

## Layer 1 — Alma (Soul)

The brand's internal engine. Everything the audience eventually sees is downstream of this layer.

### Step 1 · Charismatic Leader

Define the person behind the brand. Extract:

- **Backstory** — the experience that earned the right to lead this tribe.
- **Character flaws** — the humanising imperfections that create relatability. Perfect leaders repel; flawed-but-growing leaders attract.
- **Polarising point of view** — what do you believe that the mainstream rejects? (Prolific Index: different enough to be noticed, plausible enough to be bought.)
- **Identity label** — a name your followers can wear ("I'm a Funnel Hacker", "I'm building in public").

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → Attractive Character; `tribes.md` → Leader archetype; `fundamentals-of-personal-branding.md` → ICP + positioning worksheets.

### Step 2 · Future-Based Cause

The movement, not the product. Requires four pieces:

1. **Slogan** — a single sentence that encodes the transformation ("You're just one funnel away from…").
2. **4-Minute Mile** — proof that someone like the audience has already crossed this threshold.
3. **Self-identification label** — what members call themselves.
4. **Title of Liberty** — a mini-manifesto or manifesto video that makes the cause real.

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → The Cause; `tribes.md` → Anatomy of a Movement (Bill Bradley's three elements).

### Step 3 · New Opportunity

Frame the offer as a *new vehicle*, not an improvement to an existing one. Improvement offers force the prospect to admit past failure (status loss). A new opportunity sells *away* from pain into a fresh start.

- **Opportunity Switch** — move them from an old vehicle to yours (first sale).
- **Opportunity Stack** — additional new opportunities *within* your world (every subsequent sale).

Drill the niche: Hot Market → Submarket → **Unique Value Zone** (where expertise, passion, and a painful monetisable problem intersect). Do not position in the middle of an existing niche — carve a new one.

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → The New Opportunity + niche carving; `iman-ghazi-the-one-thing.md` → Third Layer Theory (UVZ); `we-are-all-weird.md` → Four Forces for Weird (why the niche exists).

### Step 4 · Big Domino

Reduce the entire brand to one belief statement:

> "If I can make people believe that **[new opportunity]** is the key to **[what they desire most]** and is only attainable through **[my specific vehicle]**, then all other objections become irrelevant."

One domino. If you have two, pick one. Everything downstream — content, offers, webinars — is engineered to knock this single domino over.

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → Secret #5.

### Step 5 · Origin Story / Epiphany Bridge

The emotional aha that earns belief in the Big Domino. Logic doesn't sell; epiphanies do. Use the 9-step script:

1. **Backstory** — where you were before.
2. **External desire** — what you wanted to achieve.
3. **Internal desire** — what you wanted to feel.
4. **Wall** — the moment your old approach failed.
5. **Epiphany** — the insight that changed everything.
6. **New opportunity** — the vehicle the epiphany revealed.
7. **Plan** — the steps you took.
8. **Conflict** — the obstacles you hit.
9. **Achievement → Transformation** — the external result and who you became.

Keep language at a conversational level ("kinda like a…" bridges). Technobabble before the epiphany kills the sale.

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → Epiphany Bridge + Hero's Two Journeys.

### Step 6 · Three False Beliefs

Every prospect arrives with three categories of objection that block the Big Domino. Identify and story-bust each one:

- **Vehicle belief** — "This approach won't work." (Break with a case study or proof story.)
- **Internal belief** — "I can't do it / I'm not the type." (Break with a transformation story.)
- **External belief** — "Outside forces will stop me." (Break with a situational story.)

Present each as a "Secret" — a curiosity-hook framing that turns a blocker into a promise.

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → Secrets 9–10; `tribes.md` → Fear of criticism (name the fear explicitly).

### Step 7 · Method / Framework

Your proprietary system — the named process that delivers the transformation. Give it a unique name (Unique Profit Branding). A named framework:

- Creates instant recognisability.
- Signals rigour and repeatability.
- Becomes a tribe artefact — members reference it, share it, debate it.

Structure it as a Visceral Transformation Map: Before → After in 3–5 stages, each with a clear objective, key tasks, milestones, and tools.

*Refs:* `iman-ghazi-the-one-thing.md` → Synthesize Map + Visceral Transformation Map; `expert-secrets.md` → Stack Slide structure.

### Step 8 · Brand Message / Manifesto

Consolidate Pillars 1–7 into a single articulation:

- **One-liner:** Who you serve + what you help them do + the unique vehicle.
- **Manifesto:** A short declaration of the cause, the enemy (status quo), and the future you're building toward. This is the tribe's sacred text.

Publish the manifesto before you publish anything else. It is the first artefact of the movement.

*Refs:* `expert-secrets.md` → Title of Liberty; `tribes.md` → Micromovement recipe (publish a manifesto is action #1).

## Layer 2 — Código (Primal Code)

The structural scaffolding that makes the brand legible and stickable. Audit all 7 pieces from Hanlon's Primal Code:

| Piece | Question to answer |
|---|---|
| **Creation Story** | Why did this brand come to exist? What struggle or epiphany created it? |
| **Creed** | What does the brand believe? (3–5 declarative statements.) |
| **Icons** | What objects, symbols, or images are instantly associated with this brand? |
| **Rituals** | What do members *do* together or repeatedly? (Onboarding, check-ins, community gestures.) |
| **Pagans** | Who is explicitly not the audience? What is the enemy, the status quo, the thing the tribe rallied against? |
| **Sacred Words** | What language is unique to this brand? (Named frameworks, internal vocabulary, slogans.) |
| **Leader** | How is the leader visible, fallible, and human? Not a logo — a person. |

A brand is only as strong as its weakest Primal Code piece. A missing Creed means the tribe has no shared belief; missing Rituals means members can't connect with each other.

*Refs:* `primal-branding.md` → 7-piece system; `tribes.md` → four communication flows (leader-to-tribe, tribe-to-leader, member-to-member, member-to-outsider); tighten before you grow.

### Archetype overlay

Once the 7 pieces are drafted, assign a **primary archetype** (and optionally a secondary) using the Two-Axes quadrant from Mark & Pearson:

| Quadrant | Archetypes |
|---|---|
| Freedom + Independence | Innocent, Explorer, Sage |
| Freedom + Mastery | Hero, Outlaw, Magician |
| Order + Belonging | Regular Guy/Gal, Lover, Jester |
| Order + Independence | Caregiver, Creator, Ruler |

The archetype should align with:
1. The leader's natural character (not an aspirational persona — the one that's already there).
2. The category's dominant archetype — then decide to own it or counter-position.
3. The audience's dominant desire and wound.

Check the Brand Bank: is the chosen archetype already owned in this category by a dominant competitor? If so, either out-execute them with greater consistency or choose a gap.

*Refs:* `the-hero-and-the-outlaw.md` → Two Axes, 12-archetype table, Brand Bank, Artichoke 5 Steps.

## Layer 3 — Corpo (Body)

Only built after Layers 1 and 2 are solid. The Body is the visible expression of the Soul and the Code — not decoration applied on top.

### Content strategy

- **Platform selection:** One platform, one year. Pick where your ICP lives AND where your natural format (long-form video / written / short-form) aligns. Do not branch until 1,000 engaged followers.
- **Content Ecosystem:** Foundations → Brand Identity → Content Strategy → Creation & Distribution → Optimisation. Do not jump to distribution before identity is clear.
- **5-Stage Content Cycle:** Conflict → Internal Pursuit → Action → Result → Content. The best content documents genuine tension, not curated success.
- **Three Value Pillars:** Documentation (honest journey), Opening Someone's Eyes (reframe a belief), Education (solve a specific problem). Every piece of content should serve at least one.
- **Mine-field Farming over virality:** Map 5–10 audience pain points. Write 2 framing angles per pain point (problem-oriented + solution-oriented). Build searchable, compounding content — not viral bets.
- **7-11-4 Authority Rule:** Before someone buys, they need 7 hours of consumption across 11 touch-points in 4 different locations. Consistency over time is the only path.
- **Repurposing engine:** Twitter → LinkedIn → YouTube Script → LinkedIn Newsletter → Instagram Clips → TikTok/LinkedIn redistribution. One core idea, six outputs.

*Refs:* `iman-ghazi-the-one-thing.md` → Content Ecosystem, Mine-field Farming, Repurposing; `fundamentals-of-personal-branding.md` → platform strategy + ICP.

### Monetisation (MVDP)

A Minimum Viable Digital Product converts brand authority into scalable income. Use the Synthesize Map sequence:

1. Confirm UVZ (Third Layer Theory — Industry → Niche → Sub-niche).
2. Problem Farming: score problems across Pain / Worsening / Purchasing Power / Speed / Willingness.
3. Test commercial viability before building.
4. Build a Visceral Transformation Map (Before → After, 3–5 stages).
5. Develop Action Plan and tools (worksheets, checklists, named frameworks).
6. Craft Brand Monetisation Offer.
7. Create Unique Profit Branding (named framework/vehicle — links back to Layer 1 Method).
8. Write MVDP Charter (one-pager: name, goal, structure, delivery, expected results).
9. Load into delivery platform.
10. **Sell high-ticket first.** Validate with 3–5 high-ticket clients before building a course or community at scale.

*Refs:* `iman-ghazi-the-one-thing.md` → Synthesize Map; `expert-secrets.md` → Stack Slide + 4-Question Close for high-ticket.

## Operating rules

- **Layer order is non-negotiable.** If the user asks for a logo, tagline, or content calendar before the Soul is defined, name the gap and ask to complete Layer 1 first.
- **One platform, one year.** Do not plan multi-platform presence for a brand that does not yet have 1,000 engaged followers on any single platform.
- **Sell the new opportunity, not the improvement.** Every offer framing check: does this force the prospect to admit past failure? If yes, reframe.
- **Virality ≠ revenue.** A small qualified audience outperforms a large unqualified one. Do not optimise for reach before authority is established.
- **The manifesto ships first.** Before any product, before any funnel — the tribe needs a declaration of what it believes.
- **Stay in the Prolific Zone.** If the brand position feels safe and mainstream, it won't be noticed. If it feels delusional, it won't be bought. Find the edge between.

## Anti-patterns to call out

| Pattern | Why it fails | Redirect |
|---|---|---|
| "I want to appeal to everyone" | Mass is dead. Serving everyone means the brand is irrelevant to anyone. | Use the Four Words (mass/normal/weird/rich) to reframe; pick a tribe's normal. |
| Starting with visuals | A logo without a Soul is decoration without meaning. | Complete the Big Domino and Manifesto first. |
| Generic content | If the post could have been written by anyone, it builds no authority. | Anchor every piece to a specific conflict from the 5-Stage Content Cycle. |
| Launching straight to course | A course without validated demand is a product nobody asked for. | Run Problem Farming and sell 3–5 high-ticket clients first. |
| Copying other creators | "The best content is created as a result of internal conflict." — Iman Ghazi | Source content from genuine tension, not from successful templates. |
| Updating the vehicle every 90 days | Authority takes 7 hours × 11 touch-points × 4 platforms — time is the only path. | Run the same core offer weekly for at least a year before automating. |

## Delivery sequence

When executing a full brand build, follow this order:

1. **Soul audit** (Pillars 1–8) — 1 working session per pillar; do not rush.
2. **Primal Code audit** (7 pieces) — identify which are missing or weak.
3. **Archetype selection** (Two-Axes quadrant + Brand Bank check).
4. **Manifesto draft** — publish this before anything else.
5. **Platform selection + Content Ecosystem setup** — one platform, 90-day plan.
6. **MVDP validation** — Problem Farming → high-ticket pilot → then scale.
