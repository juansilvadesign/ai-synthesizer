## TL;DR

Vexa's M.R.C. (Multiplicador de Receita Comercial) is a high-performance B2B closing system designed to replace the traditional 3-month sales cycle with a single call close. It swaps recurring-subscription dependency for upfront implementation pricing, positions the offer as a unique transformative mechanism (not a commodity service), and runs the closer through a strict sequence — rapport → GPCT BA CI qualification → pitch → anchoring → tiered negotiation — so every call ends with a clear yes or no.

---

## When to use

Use this guide when running or preparing a high-ticket B2B consultative sales call where the goal is to close in the meeting itself, not hand off to a follow-up sequence.

## Prerequisites

- Product structured as M.R.C.: unique mechanism name, transformation promise, and micro-solutions packaged inside the core offer.
- Anchor price prepared (4–8× table value) and table value (VT) defined; protagonist value (VP) is VT minus up to 30%.
- Downsell product ready and rehearsed before the call — never improvised.
- Bonuses pre-planned (high perceived value, low effort to deliver — not tied to your hours).
- GPCT BA CI question bank mapped to your niche.
- Negotiation lines written out in advance (never improvise discounts).

## Procedure

1. **Open with rapport** (2–5 min). Greet by name, find one genuine point of connection (city, hobby, recent post). Mirror tone and pace. Smile. Keep it a two-way exchange, not a monologue.
2. **Ask the opening question.** "O que fez você dedicar seu tempo hoje para estar aqui? O que você quer resolver?" — this surfaces buying intent early.
3. **Deliver personal branding positioning.** In 60 seconds: your background, what you've proven, why you're the right person to help.
4. **Set Intent Agreement #1.** Align on call structure: "I'm not here to sell you anything now — I'm here to understand your business. If we conclude I can help, I'll present a tailored solution. Can we agree on that?"
   - **If** client tries to skip ahead to price, **then** hold the structure and lower their Sales Shield — never skip qualification.
5. **Run GPCT BA CI qualification** (10–20 min). Follow this exact order — always end on the positive pole:
   - **Goals** — paint a vivid scene ("On Dec 31 with your family, what do you want to have achieved?").
   - **Plans** — expose the gap between ambition and current action. Let the client conclude they can't get there alone.
   - **Challenges** — ask what's actually blocking them day-to-day.
   - **Timeline** — rate urgency 1–5; if below 3, ask what's missing for a 5.
   - **Budget** — ask what they'd expect to invest; ask what they've invested previously. Detect hidden financial stress — don't hammer on money if the client is clearly strained.
   - **Authority** — confirm all decision-makers are on the call. If not, don't proceed — reschedule.
   - **Consequences** — move to a negative scenario. "If you keep doing what you're doing today, what happens in 6 months?" Generate urgency.
   - **Implications** — end on the positive. "If we solve this together and you hit that target, what changes in your life?" (Peak-end rule: always finish qualification high.)
6. **Set Intent Agreement #2 (transition to pitch).** "Based on everything you told me, I'm confident we can help. Let me show you our work — then we'll decide together if it makes sense. Deal?"
7. **Deliver the pitch — methodology first, product second.**
   - Present company/professional credibility (Ethos).
   - Show the methodology: the before/after context, why it works. Work all three persuasion channels: visual (slides, social proof tsunami), auditory (conviction, tone, pauses), kinesthetic (future-self visualisation).
   - Mid-pitch: ask "De 1 a 5, o quanto você acredita que isso consegue te ajudar?" to take temperature.
   - Set Intent Agreement #3: "If I show you a solution that truly fits your situation, can I count on your yes or your no today?"
   - Present product: frame every deliverable as a transformation, not a task list. Use mockups. Never read a list of features.
8. **Anchor price.** Present a value-of-record that is 4–8× the table value. Show a full-price tally ("If you could get 75% off everything here, would you be a client today?"). Never use internal terms (VT, VP, ancoragem) out loud.
9. **Present table value (VT).** Replay product highlights + social proof cascade. Flood with case stories. Associate each proof to the client's stated goal.
10. **Run the 7–12 rule.** State the price → "Isso aqui funciona para você?" → stop screen share → count 7–12 seconds silently. Let discomfort work.
    - **If** client says yes, **then** proceed to payment immediately.
    - **If** client objects, **then** begin negotiation sequence below.
11. **Sustain table value (Close #1).** Defend the price — don't offer a discount immediately. Use qualification data as ammunition. Isolate objections until only financial remains: "É só preço que te impede fechar hoje?"
12. **Present differentiated condition / protagonist value (Close #2).** Justify *why* this special price exists, set a 30-day expiry, and get an explicit agreement that the price expires at the end of the call: "Ficou claro que saindo dessa call, essa condição não vale mais?"
    - **If** client closes, **then** proceed to payment.
13. **Add bonus (Close #3).** Drop one high-perceived-value / low-effort bonus. "Se fizer em 12x sem juros, fica melhor para você?"
    - **If** client closes, **then** proceed to payment.
14. **Add product bonus or downsell (Close #4).** Drop a simpler product variant. Present quickly using the same methodology. Show a clear visual comparison between product 1 and product 2 so the gap in value is obvious.
    - **If** client enters product 2, **then** proceed to payment.
15. **Run 8-24-7 strategy (last resort).** Offer: 8 hours to decide, 24 hours to sign the contract, 7-day money-back guarantee. Reframe: "You don't need to pay now — just sign. And if anything changes in 7 days, you get a full refund. The price you see today is the cheapest it will ever be."
16. **Collect payment on the call.** Send link in chat or WhatsApp. Switch to low-pressure rapport filler (talk about their city, ask about the onboarding schedule) while the client completes payment. Never stop the call early. Never trust "I'll buy when I get home."
17. **Book onboarding call.** The onboarding is where the client truly buys in. Treat it as seriously as the sales call.

## Watch out for

- Skipping the qualification because the client seems eager — always run the structure. A high-temperature lead still needs their objections surfaced before the close.
- Starting negotiation at your minimum price. Always open at anchor → table value → protagonist value. Never lead with the floor.
- Using internal pricing terms (VT, VP, ancoragem) aloud — the client must perceive every price as real.
- Letting the client take control of the call. The closer always drives. If the client dominates, they'll treat you as a subordinate vendor, not a partner.
- Adding unplanned bonuses mid-call. All bonuses must be designed in advance; improvised bonuses destroy perceived value.
- Ending a call without a decision. "I'll think about it" is a no. Always get yes or no before hanging up.
- Ignoring the authority check. If a co-decision-maker exists and isn't on the call, reschedule.

## Frameworks / models

- **M.R.C. (Multiplicador de Receita Comercial)** — single-offer system where the offer is positioned as a unique mechanism (not a commodity service), sold via an upfront implementation fee rather than a recurring subscription.
- **GPCT BA CI** — qualification sequence: Goals → Plans → Challenges → Timeline → Budget → Authority → Consequences → Implications. Always end on Implications (positive pole).
- **Ethos / Pathos / Logos × Visual / Auditory / Kinesthetic** — 3×3 persuasion matrix. Each pillar of trust (credibility, emotion, logic) is activated through three sensory channels in every call stage.
- **Price ladder: Anchor → VT → VP** — anchor (4–8× real value) creates reference pain; table value (VT) triggers the close attempt; protagonist value (VP, ≤30% off VT) is the genuine offer.
- **Product stack: Tripwire → Core → Premium** — tripwire capitalises every call; core is the scalable main offer; premium generates cash in early stages.
- **8-24-7 strategy** — last-resort close: 8h to decide, 24h to sign, 7-day guarantee removes perceived risk.
- **Peak-end rule** — qualification must end on a positive (Implications), not Consequences. Emotional memory of the call shapes the buying decision.
