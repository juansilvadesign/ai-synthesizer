# Photography Lighting

> Source: [Photography Lighting Explained: How to Use Different Lights Like a Pro - Aftershoot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUdJkAI4avc)

This guide breaks down essential lighting techniques for video and photography, based on professional practices explained in the video.

## Core Lighting Components

Understanding these basic light types is the foundation of any professional setup:

- **Key Light (0:42):** The "boss" of your lighting. It is the primary light source used to illuminate your subject.
- **Fill Light (1:30):** Used to tone down or eliminate shadows created by the key light. You can use an actual light or simply bounce the key light.
- **Backlight (2:04):** Positioned behind the subject to create an outline, helping to separate the subject from the background.
- **Hair Light (2:18):** A variation of the backlight positioned higher to specifically illuminate the subject’s head/hair.
- **Background Light (2:46):** A light source dedicated to illuminating the background, adding depth or detail to the scene.

## Lighting Techniques and Setups

By combining the core lights above, you can achieve specific visual moods and effects:

1. **Split Lighting (3:23):** Placing the key light to the side of the subject, splitting the face into light and shadow. This is ideal for **drama and depth**.
2. **Butterfly Lighting (3:47):** Positioning the key light in front and slightly above the subject. It creates a "butterfly"-shaped shadow under the nose.
3. **Clamshell Lighting (4:25):** A two-light setup with a key light above the eyes and a fill light directly under the subject. This creates **soft, flattering portraits**.
4. **Rembrandt Lighting (4:57):** Placing the key light 45° away from the subject and slightly above eye level. This creates a classic, professional look known for its characteristic triangle of light on the shadowed cheek.
