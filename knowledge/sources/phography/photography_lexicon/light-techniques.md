# Flash Portrait Backlighting Techniques

> Source: [Ed Verosky](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fx29YjcC9vc)

This guide covers the fundamentals of using backlighting to enhance depth, dimension, and visual interest in portrait photography, as presented by *Ed Verosky*.

## Core Definition

**Backlighting** is a lighting technique where a light source is positioned behind or to the rear of the subject, directed toward the camera. It is a powerful tool to outline the subject, separate them from the background, and add a sense of drama (0:07-0:54).

## Key Techniques

### 1. Halo Effect

- **Description:** A classic effect (often associated with 80s/90s photography) that creates an ethereal, glowing perimeter around the subject's hair (1:25-1:47).
- **Usage:** Can be achieved using natural sunlight or a dedicated flash positioned directly behind the subject (1:47-2:07, 3:34-3:52).

### 2. Hair Light

- **Description:** A more subtle, traditional approach focused specifically on illuminating the hair to add separation between the subject and the background (4:00-4:35).
- **Setup:** Often part of a classic three-point lighting setup, usually positioned above the subject or slightly to the side/rear (4:36-5:25).

### 3. Rim / Kicker Light

- **Description:** Creates a narrow highlight around the edges or contours of the subject’s body to emphasize their outline (5:35-5:55).
- **Setup:** Uses strip softboxes or flashes coming from the side/rear. Using two lights (one on each side) can create a distinct contour highlight on both sides, making the subject "pop" from the background (6:31-7:22).

## Creative Effects and Common Pitfalls

- **Blowing out the edges:** Using strong light to create a wraparound, glowy effect (7:50-8:23).
- **Lens Flare:** Caused by light hitting the lens directly. Can be avoided by "flagging" (blocking) the light path or embraced for an artistic, sunny look (8:25-9:05).
- **Silhouettes:** Achieved by exclusively using a strong backlight without a front-side key light, resulting in a dark subject against a bright background (9:06-9:37).
